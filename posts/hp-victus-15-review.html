My HP Victus 15 Gaming Experience After 6 Months

April 1, 2026 8 min read By Anjana Wijerathna

Six months ago, I made the decision to invest in a gaming laptop for university and gaming. Here's everything I've learned about the HP Victus 15 - the good, the bad, and everything in between.

Why I Chose the HP Victus 15

When I was looking for a gaming laptop, I had a few key requirements. As a software engineering student, I needed something that could handle development work - running IDEs, multiple browsers, databases, and sometimes Docker. But I also wanted to game on it, primarily playing Valorant, GTA V, and occasionally some Minecraft.

My budget was tight - I'm a student in Sri Lanka, and gaming laptops here can be expensive due to import taxes and currency exchange rates. After months of research, I settled on the HP Victus 15-fb2063dx because it offered the best value for money at its price point.

The specs that sold me were the AMD Ryzen 5 7535HS processor paired with the RX 6550M GPU, 16GB DDR5 RAM, and that beautiful 144Hz display. For the price, I couldn't find anything that matched these specifications.

First Impressions and Unboxing

The moment I unboxed the HP Victus 15, I was impressed by its design. HP has moved away from the aggressive gaming aesthetics of their older Omen line and gone for something more subtle. The Victus has a clean, minimalist look that could pass for a regular productivity laptop in a coffee shop.

The laptop came with Windows 11 pre-installed, which was great since I didn't have to deal with the Windows 10 to 11 upgrade headache. Setup was straightforward, though I did spend the first evening bloatware-removing and configuring everything to my liking.

Opening the lid reveals the 15.6-inch FHD display with thin bezels. The RGB keyboard backlight is a nice touch, though I wish HP had gone with per-key RGB instead of just the single-color zones. Still, it's functional and looks decent in low-light conditions.

Daily Performance for Development Work

As a software engineering student, my daily workflow involves VS Code, multiple Chrome tabs (because let's be honest, who doesn't have 30+ tabs open?), Docker Desktop, and sometimes a local MySQL or MongoDB instance running.

The Ryzen 5 7535HS handles all of this with ease. The 6 cores and 12 threads mean I can compile code, run tests, and browse documentation without any slowdown. The 16GB of DDR5 RAM is sufficient for most tasks, though I've occasionally felt the pinch when running multiple Docker containers simultaneously.

One thing I've noticed is how well the laptop manages thermal throttling. Under heavy development workloads, the fans do spin up, but the laptop maintains good performance without the dramatic speed drops I've experienced on other laptops in this price range. The thermal management is genuinely impressive for a laptop this thin.

Gaming Performance: What Can It Handle?

This is where things get interesting. I've tested various games on this laptop, and here's a breakdown of what to expect:

Valorant: This is my most-played game, and the Victus handles it beautifully. I consistently get 200+ FPS on medium settings, which is more than enough for competitive play. The 144Hz display makes a noticeable difference compared to my old 60Hz monitor.

GTA V: Running GTA V at high settings with a mix of shadows and reflections, I get around 60-70 FPS in the city and 80+ FPS in less demanding areas. The RX 6550M surprises me with its performance here.

Minecraft: With shaders installed, Minecraft runs smoothly. I've been playing with RTX shaders disabled but with reasonable render distance and texture packs, and it stays above 60 FPS without issues.

Fortnite: Epic settings at 1080p give me around 60-80 FPS, which is more than playable. Dropping to medium settings pushes this above 100 FPS.

The Display: More Than Just Numbers

On paper, the 144Hz FHD IPS display sounds standard. In practice, it's one of the highlights of this laptop. Colors are vibrant, viewing angles are excellent, and the 144Hz refresh rate makes everything feel smoother - not just gaming, but scrolling through web pages and general UI interactions too.

For content consumption, whether it's watching YouTube videos or Netflix, the display delivers a pleasant experience. It's not OLED-level black levels, but for a laptop in this price bracket, it's more than satisfactory.

Battery Life: The Reality

I'll be honest - the battery life is where compromises were made. During development work with moderate screen brightness, I get around 4-5 hours. Gaming, of course, requires the charger - expect 1.5-2 hours max.

The 52.5Wh battery is decent but not exceptional. I always carry my charger to university, which isn't ideal but is the reality of gaming laptops. When I'm working away from a power outlet, I've learned to close unnecessary applications and reduce brightness to extend battery life.

What Could Be Better

No laptop is perfect, and the Victus has its share of minor annoyances:

  • Speaker Quality: The speakers are functional but tinny. For gaming or watching movies, I'd recommend headphones or external speakers.
  • Webcam: The 720p webcam is usable for video calls but nothing special. In low light, the quality drops significantly.
  • Storage: 512GB feels cramped quickly. I've had to manage storage carefully, and an external SSD is now part of my setup.
  • Fan Noise: Under load, the fans can get noticeable. It's not terrible, but it's definitely present.

Final Verdict: Worth It?

After six months of daily use, I can confidently say the HP Victus 15 was the right choice for me. It delivers solid performance for both development work and gaming, has a beautiful display, and hasn't given me any major issues.

The value proposition is strong - you're getting a laptop that can handle university work and gaming without breaking the bank. Yes, there are better laptops out there, but they'd cost significantly more.

If you're a student in Sri Lanka or similar market looking for a gaming laptop that won't disappoint, the HP Victus 15 should be on your shortlist. It's not flashy, but it's reliable, capable, and represents excellent value for money.

Would I buy it again? Absolutely.
