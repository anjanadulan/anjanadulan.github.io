My Gaming Desk Tour 2026

February 20, 2026 5 min read

People always ask about my gaming setup, so I figured I'd do a proper desk tour for 2026. This is the setup I use daily for both gaming and software development. It's evolved over the years, and I'm pretty happy with where it's at now.

The Main Machine: HP Victus 15

The centerpiece of my setup is my HP Victus 15 gaming laptop. I picked this up last year and it's been a solid performer:

  • Processor: AMD Ryzen 5 7535HS
  • Graphics: AMD RX 6550M 4GB
  • Memory: 16GB DDR5
  • Storage: 512GB SSD
  • Display: 15.6" FHD 144Hz

The Ryzen 5 processor handles development work beautifully - running VS Code, multiple browser tabs, Docker containers, and everything else I need for software development without breaking a sweat. For gaming, the RX 6550M pushes most titles at medium to high settings, and the 144Hz display makes everything feel smooth.

I know it's not the most powerful laptop on the market, but for the price-to-performance ratio in Sri Lanka, it was the right choice. More on my full review in my previous post.

The Input Devices

Mouse: Gamdias Zeus E2

My go-to mouse is the Gamdias Zeus E2. I've tried various mice over the years, but this one just feels right in my hand. The sensor is accurate, the clicks are responsive, and the RGB lighting adds a nice touch without being too distracting.

For FPS games like Valorant, precision matters. The Zeus E2 delivers that. The weight is balanced, and I can make quick flick shots without worrying about the mouse feeling sluggish.

Keyboard: Gamdias Ares M2

Mechanical keyboard enthusiast? Not exactly, but I do appreciate good tactile feedback. The Gamdias Ares M2 comes with Outemu Blue switches, which are clicky and satisfying. Yes, my roommates probably hate me, but there's something therapeutic about the clicking.

The full-size layout includes a numpad, which comes in handy for spreadsheets and occasionally for keybinds in games. The build quality is solid for the price, and the aluminum frame gives it some heft so it doesn't slide around during intense gaming sessions.

Audio: Fantech Orbit HG25

Sound is crucial for competitive gaming. The Fantech Orbit HG25 headset provides good audio quality without breaking the bank. The microphone is clear enough for Discord calls and in-game chat, and the ear cups are comfortable enough for extended gaming sessions.

I'm not an audiophile, but I appreciate good spatial audio for games. The HG25 gives me enough positional awareness in FPS games to know where footsteps are coming from.

The Desktop PC (Secondary Rig)

I also have a desktop setup at home, mainly for when I'm not traveling or need more screen real estate:

  • CPU: AMD Ryzen 3 3200G with Vega 8 iGPU
  • RAM: 16GB
  • Storage: 2TB HDD

This isn't a gaming powerhouse - the Vega 8 iGPU handles light gaming and older titles, but it's my go-to for video editing and general tasks when I don't want to drain my laptop's battery. Plus, it's a good learning machine for experimenting with different OS configurations.

Desk Organization

My desk is a simple wooden surface with enough room for the laptop, a drink coaster, and some essentials. I'm not one of those people with perfectly cable-managed setups and LED strips everywhere (though that does look cool). I prefer function over form when it comes to my workspace.

One thing I do prioritize is having a dedicated space for my water bottle. Hydration is important during long gaming or coding sessions, and having a spill-proof container near the keyboard is essential.

What's Next?

Looking at potential upgrades for the future:

  • An external monitor for the laptop setup - the 15.6" display is fine but a 27" would be nice
  • A proper desk pad to replace the makeshift mousepad
  • A webcam for streaming and video calls
  • Maybe a capture card for console gaming content

But honestly, the current setup serves me well. It's not about having the best gear - it's about knowing your gear and making the most of what you have. I've won (and lost) plenty of Valorant matches with this setup, and I've shipped code from this desk that I'm proud of.

If you have questions about specific peripherals or want recommendations for similar gear, feel free to reach out!
